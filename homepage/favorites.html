My Favorite Bollywood Classics

3 Idiots

3 Idiots is a 2009 Indian Hindi-language coming-of-age comedy-drama. The film follows the friendship of three students at an Indian engineering college and is a satire about the social pressures under an Indian education system. The film is narrated through parallel dramas, one in the present and the other ten years in the past. Aamir Khan as Rancho plays the role of a very bright student who motivates his friends pursue their passion and make a career out of it. R.Madhavan as Farhan who wants to become a wildlife photographer but was forced to pursue engineering because of his strict father. Overall the film depicts a very nice mingle of comedy, suspense and motivational. All the actors and co-actors done a commendable job. Some of the scenes are really emotional. This is one of my favorite Bollywood movies.

Title Translation: 3 Idiots

Kal Ho Na Ho

Kal Ho Naa Ho is a 2003 Indian Hindi-language romantic comedy-drama. It tells the story of Naina Catherine Kapur, a pessimistic and uptight MBA student, living in New York City. She falls in love with her neighbour Aman Mathur, a terminally ill patient who tries to set Naina up with her best friend Rohit Patel, since he fears she will grieve for him if he reciprocates her feelings. The movie employed actual ethnic residents of Queens as extras, and depicted Indian and Indian-American characters as an organic part of everyday American life and culture. It even portrayed everyday American capitalism: A major subplot involves Jennifer’s competition with a neighboring restaurant.

Title Translation: Tomorrow May Never Come

Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani

Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani is a 2013 Indian Hindi-language coming-of-age romantic comedy. This movie is a story of the exhilarating and terrifying journey of four characters as they navigate through their youth; from their carefree laughter as they set off on a holiday together in their college days, until their bittersweet tears as they watch the first of their friends get married. Naina Talwar (protagonist) makes an impulsive decision to go on a hiking trip into the Himalayas, up to Manali. Before Naina can express herself, Kabir leaves India to pursue his career. They meet again years later, but he still cherishes his dreams more than bonds.

Title Translation: This Youth Is Crazy

Dear Zindagi

Dear Zindagi is a 2016 Indian Hindi-language coming-of-age drama film. Kaira, a young girl, faces problems in her relationships. Things get complicated and Kaira has to deal with the low phase in his personal and professional life. She moves back to her parents in Goa for a short time. Out there she meets a physiatrist Jehangir Khan (Shahrukh Khan) and starts following his therapies so that she can lead a normal and peaceful life. He helps her resolve her issues with her parents and get a new perspective on life, which leads to her following her passion and an improvement in her love life. Not only does the movie aim to break through the taboo that surrounds mental illness but it also gives out major life lessons: let it go, parents can make mistakes, the harder path might not always be the right one, it is okay not to be okay, and asking for help does not make you weak.

Title Translation: Dear life

Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge

Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge also known by DDLJ, is a 1995 Indian Hindi-language romance film. The story of the film set in 90s revolves around two strangers who happen to bump into each other during a Europe trip. Raj and Simran, the two immortal characters have a hate-hate relationship which later turns into undying love. However, Simran’s father has vowed to get his girl married to his friend’s son and so the family leaves for India. This is the time when the love dawns upon the two estranged friends and Raj sets out to win back his love. “DDLJ" has such widespread popularity because it manages to convey two entirely opposing messages. It argues that we should follow our hearts and chase happiness wherever it leads, regardless of the obstacles in our paths, while simultaneously suggesting we should respect the ways of our elders, particularly our parents, and do nothing that challenges their will.

Title Translation: The Big-Hearted Will Take the Bride

Kabhi Khushi Kabhi Gham

Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham is a classic also known as K3G, is a 2001 Indian Hindi-language melodrama film. Rahul (Shah Rukh Khan), the adoptive son of business magnate, feels eternal gratitude to his father for rescuing him from a life of poverty. Yet, when Yash forbids his love of poor Anjali (Kajol), Rahul marries her and moves to London with new wife and sister-in-law, breaking the heart of his mother. Ten years later, Rahul's younger brother comes to London intent on brokering peace between father and son. The movies portrays that elders also make mistakes, social status does not define a person/family, our family is our biggest strength and weakness, and time will heal.

Title Translation: Sometimes there is joy, sometimes there is sorrow
